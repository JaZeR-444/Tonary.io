# Tonary — Target User

> Who Tonary is for, what they need, and who it is explicitly not for.
> Related: [product-principles.md](product-principles.md) · [mvp-scope.md](mvp-scope.md) · [terminology.md](terminology.md)

Tonary serves FL Studio producers and sound designers who own more plugins, presets, and workflow knowledge than they can hold in their head — and who work increasingly on mobile, in and out of the studio.

## Primary Personas

### 1. The FL Studio Producer / Beatmaker — "Marcus"
- **Context:** Makes beats daily in FL Studio; phone always in hand, often away from the DAW (commute, couch, sessions elsewhere).
- **Needs:** Fast recall of "which plugin/preset did that sound"; quick comparisons before committing; save ideas mid-scroll to apply later.
- **Pains:** Preset paralysis; forgets go-to chains; loses track across 100+ plugins; desktop tools don't travel.
- **Success:** Opens Tonary on mobile, finds the right tool/patch in seconds, and returns to FL Studio knowing the next move.

### 2. The Sound Designer — "Priya"
- **Context:** Crafts and catalogs signature sounds; cares about *why* a patch works, not just that it does.
- **Needs:** Evidence-backed notes tied to sources (Tonary Review); structured parameter and preset records (Tonary Vault); reproducible setup paths (Tonary Flow).
- **Pains:** Scattered notes across apps; no trustworthy single record; hard to compare two approaches rigorously.
- **Success:** A curated, sourced library she trusts and can reason over — comparisons she can defend.

### 3. The Plugin-Heavy Creator — "Dre"
- **Context:** Owns a large, sprawling plugin collection; constantly evaluating new tools vs. what he already has.
- **Needs:** Recommendations and matching (Tonary Scout); fast comparisons and next-step suggestions (Tonary Briefs); a way to decide *before* buying/loading.
- **Pains:** Overlapping plugins, decision fatigue, "do I already own something that does this?"
- **Success:** Confident buy/skip and load decisions; fewer redundant plugins; less time lost to evaluation.

## Shared Needs Across Personas
- Organize plugin/preset/workflow knowledge that currently lives in memory or scattered files.
- Understand and compare choices quickly.
- Recall and apply prior decisions.
- Do all of the above on a phone, offline, in a premium calm interface.

## Contexts of Use
- **In-studio (second screen):** phone beside the DAW for recall and reference.
- **On-the-go:** commute, couch, other studios — capturing and browsing offline.
- **Pre-purchase / pre-load:** deciding what to use before opening the plugin.

## What Success Looks Like
- Time-to-recall a plugin/preset drops to seconds.
- Users trust Tonary records enough to act on them.
- Fewer redundant purchases; more finished ideas.

## Non-Users (out of scope)
- Producers on other DAWs seeking DAW-native tooling (Tonary is FL Studio–contextual, not a DAW).
- People wanting an AI chatbot as the primary experience.
- Desktop-only users unwilling to work on mobile.
- Anyone expecting audio processing / a plugin host — Tonary is an intelligence layer, not audio software, and is not affiliated with Image-Line.
